About me

My name is Trevor Stam and I’m a software developer based in Seattle. I hail from a background in GIS (geographic information systems) and economics. Although I’m an American citizen, I was born and raised in the Netherlands. I worked for several Dutch universities as an economic researcher. Last year I moved to Southern California to work in GIS and have recently moved up to the Pacific Northwest to establish myself as a developer. I steered my career in the direction of application development and away from the static sphere of academic publications and advisory reports. I would like to create applications with which users can interact, create data points or derive insights from.


I started studying geography and urban planning for my love of maps. As a kid I would spend hours leafing through atlases. I still get carried away and mesmerized at times. I aspire to incorporate my passion for maps as much as possible in the work that I’m doing. Map-based applications are gaining popularity and location-specific information is ubiquitous. Maps are visual, relatable, give you good oversight, reference and context. It might be the most inclusive medium of communicating information.


I work with technologies such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, JQuery, PostgreSQL, Express, Node.js, Heroku, EJS and Handlebars to create usable and visually pleasing applications. Furthermore I'm proficient in ArcGIS, QGIS and R. You can find more information about my projects and resume on this site. Feel free to reach out to me or connect with me via my contact page.


Next to software development and GIS, I'm an accomplished gypsy and jazz violinist. With my band I played semi-professionally across Europe. Also I'm an avid cook of all things Asian. I've added various curries to my repertoire and my kimchi is fiery (as it should be). Another passion is travelling. Experiencing boundlessness and just exploring a completely new environment is the most gratifying aspect of it all. My most invigorating travel experiences include Morocco, India, Indonesia, Japan and driving up the coast from SoCal to Seattle in my raggedy Hyundai.
